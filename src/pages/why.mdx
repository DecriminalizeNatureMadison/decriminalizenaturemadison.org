# Why should Madison decriminalize nature?

<ul id="benefits">
    <li id="mental-health-benefits">
        <Image class="icon" src={brainIcon} alt="An icon of a brain on a yellow background surrounded in blue wavy lines"/>
        <div>
            <h4>Entheogens improve mental health</h4>
            <p>
                Entheogens such as psilocybin and have been shown to alleviate symptoms of depression<a href="#footnote-1"><sup>[1]</sup></a>,
                end-of-life anxiety<a href="#footnote-2"><sup>[2]</sup></a>, and alcohol use disorder<a href="#footnote-3"><sup>[3]</sup></a>.
                Millions of American adults struggle with mental health conditions treatment-resistant depression<a href="#footnote-7"><sup>[7]</sup></a>
                and they deserve to have access to these promising medicines without needing to worry about being charged with a felony.
            </p>
        </div>
    </li>
    <li id="current-penalties">
        <Image class="icon" src={flowerBehindBarsIcon} alt="An icon of an orange flower behind jail cell bars"/>
        <div>
            <h4>Wisconsin law is incredibly harsh</h4>
            <p>
                Under current Wisconsin criminal code, almost all entheogen-related crimes are felonies <a href="#footnote-4"><sup>[4]</sup></a>
                punishable by up to 3.5 years in prison, a fine of up to $10,000 or both<a href="#footnote-4"><sup>[4]</sup></a>.
                For some entheogens such as mescaline, harvesting just 1 gram is punishable by up to 12.5 years in prison,
                a fine of up to $25,000 or both<a href="#footnote-4"><sup>[4]</sup></a>. This is despite the fact that mescaline has been used
                safely as a sacred medicine by indigenous cultures for thousands of years<a href="#footnote-5"><sup>[5]</sup></a>,
                and has recently been found to be associated with self-reported psychiatric improvements and enduring positive
                life changes<a href="#footnote-6"><sup>[6]</sup></a>.

            </p>
        </div>
    </li>
</ul>
