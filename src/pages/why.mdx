<h1 style="color: var(--action-color);">Why should Madison decriminalize nature?</h1>

<ul id="benefits" class="flex-col justify-between">
    <li id="mental-health-benefits">
        <Image class="icon" loading="eager" src={brainIcon} alt="An icon of a brain on a yellow background surrounded in blue wavy lines"/>
        <div>
            <h4>Entheogens improve mental health</h4>
            <p>
                Entheogens such as psilocybin ("magic mushrooms") have been shown to alleviate symptoms of depression<FootnoteReference index={1} />,
                end-of-life anxiety<FootnoteReference index={2} />, and alcohol use disorder<FootnoteReference index={3} />.
                Millions of American adults struggle with mental health conditions like treatment-resistant depression<FootnoteReference index={17} />,
                and they deserve to have access to these promising medicines without living under the threat of a felony.
            </p>
        </div>
    </li>
    <li id="safe-and-non-addictive">
        <Image class="icon" src={mushroomIcon} alt="An icon of two brown capped mushrooms with stems" loading="eager" />
        <div>
            <h4>Entheogens are safe and non-addictive</h4>
            <p>
                Entheogenic plants and fungi are physiologically safe and do not lead to dependence or addiction<FootnoteReference index={7} /><FootnoteReference index={8} />.
                In fact, entheogens have been shown to help break addictions such as alcohol use disorder<FootnoteReference index={3} /> and tobacco use disorder<FootnoteReference index={9} />.
                UW-Madison is even studying psilocybin ("magic mushrooms") as a way to help treat opioid use disorder<FootnoteReference index={10} />.
            </p>
        </div>
    </li>
    <li id="sense-of-community">
        <Image class="icon" loading="eager" src={heartIcon} alt="An icon of a red heart shape with a black outline"/>
        <div>
            <h4>Entheogens promote empathy and kickstart positive life change</h4>
            <p>
                Research has demonstrated that entheogenic plants and fungi can enhance feelings of empathy, self-love,
                and love for others<FootnoteReference index={12} /><FootnoteReference index={13} />. Additionally, they have
                been shown to boost the brain's neuroplasticity, allowing neural networks to adapt and grow more easily<FootnoteReference index={14} /><FootnoteReference index={15} />.
                This increased neuroplasticity enables the brain to form new connections that reinforce the positive transformations
                sparked by the experience, leading to lasting
                change<FootnoteReference index={16} />.
            </p>
        </div>
    </li>
    <li id="current-penalties">
        <Image class="icon" loading="eager" src={flowerBehindBarsIcon} alt="An icon of an orange flower behind jail cell bars"/>
        <div>
            <h4>Wisconsin law is incredibly harsh</h4>
            <p>
                Despite being non-violent, under current Wisconsin criminal code, almost all entheogen-related crimes are felonies
                punishable by up to 3.5 years in prison, a fine of up to $10,000 or both<FootnoteReference index={4} />.
                For some entheogens such as mescaline, harvesting just 1 gram is punishable by up to 12.5 years in prison,
                a fine of up to $25,000 or both<FootnoteReference index={4} />. This is despite the fact that mescaline has been used
                safely by indigenous cultures as a sacred medicine for thousands of years<FootnoteReference index={5} />.
                In fact, mescaline has recently been found to be associated with self-reported psychiatric improvements
                and lasting positive life changes<FootnoteReference index={6} />.
            </p>
        </div>
    </li>
    <li id="city-resources">
        <Image class="icon" loading="eager" src={clockIcon} alt="An icon of a clock"/>
        <div>
            <h4>Our resources are better spent elsewhere</h4>
            <p>
                The City of Madison faces many pressing issues that require its attention. By diverting resources towards
                the investigation, arrest, and prosecution of entheogen-related offenses, the city is forced to allocate
                valuable time and funds away from more critical issues. Madison is facing a 2025 budget deficit<a href="#footnote-11"><sup>[11]</sup></a>.
                Decriminalizing nature should be part of the solution.
            </p>
        </div>
    </li>
</ul>
